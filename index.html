General Strategy — Case Study

Kookoos Chain Limited

EBITDA Lift Analysis & Operational Playbook
Prepared March 31, 2026 • Data: FY2024 – Feb 2026

Executive Summary

Kookoos is a fast-growing fried chicken franchise in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania. Over 24 months, the chain has scaled from 2 locations to 7 (with 6 more planned), growing network gross revenue from 720M TZS (FY2024) to 2.45B TZS (FY2025) and reaching a ~5.8B TZS annualized run rate as of early 2026. This analysis identifies six specific EBITDA levers that can be actioned in the next 4–8 weeks, targeting a combined 180–320M TZS annual uplift ($72K–$128K USD) across the existing network.

2. Seasonality & Ramadan Impact

Key Patterns

Peak months: December is the strongest month across all branches (holiday season, end-of-year spending), followed by August–October.

Trough: February consistently underperforms, compounded by Ramadan when it overlaps (Feb 2026 saw a -9% to -30% drop vs. January across all branches except GSM Plaza).

Ramadan 2026 impact: Network revenue dropped from 511M (Jan) to 452M (Feb), a 12% hit. However, GSM Plaza actually grew +59% during Ramadan, suggesting location-specific dynamics that can be studied and replicated.

Opportunity: A targeted Ramadan menu and iftar/suhoor promotion strategy could recover 30–50M TZS of the seasonal dip.

Data Quality Issues

Across all branches and months, we identified 6+ spelling variants for what appears to be the same fries breading product ("Fries flour", "Fries Flour", "FRIES FLOUR", "Fries breading", "Fries Breading", "fries flour"). This makes aggregation unreliable and suggests a lack of standardized product masters in the POS/stock system. Fixing this alone would improve inventory visibility.

5. Same-Store Growth Deep Dive

Both mature branches show strong and sustained same-store growth throughout 2025, with Masana accelerating in Q3 (Sep: +103%, Oct: +128% YoY). The early-2025 growth was partly driven by Masana's 2024 ramp-up period creating a low base, but even late-2025 months show +21–28% YoY for Bahari, indicating genuine organic demand growth. Into Jan 2026, same-store growth settled at +38–42%, a healthy and sustainable pace.

6. Actionable EBITDA Lift Levers

The following six initiatives are ranked by estimated impact and implementation speed. Combined, they target 180–320M TZS/year of EBITDA improvement across the current 6-branch network.

1. Eliminate Breading & Fries Waste Critical — Week 1
Breading OR waste of 65% and fries waste of 24% are destroying margins. Implement batch-size controls: prepare breading in smaller quantities (halve current batch size), set fries holding time limits (7 minutes max), and introduce a simple daily waste log with manager sign-off. Train kitchen staff on demand-based prep rather than bulk prep.
Est. impact: 60–100M TZS/year (across 6 branches) • Cost to implement: Near zero (process change)
2. Standardize Product Master & Inventory System Foundation — Weeks 1–2
Create a single, canonical product list with standardized names, units, and SKU codes. Currently 6+ variants exist for the same items. This prevents accurate variance analysis and hides waste. Build a simple Google Sheet or paper template with pre-printed item names for daily stock counts. This is the foundation that makes all other improvements measurable.
Est. impact: Enables 30–50M TZS/year in identified waste savings • Cost: Staff training time only
3. Optimize Potato Procurement High Impact — Weeks 2–3
Potatoes are the #2 cost line (18% of ancillary COGS) with 4 different price tiers visible in the data (780–1,111 TZS/kg). Consolidate purchasing to the best-price supplier (EA Food at 780–900 TZS/kg vs. local market at 1,111 TZS/kg). With ~38 kg/day per branch across 6 branches, a 200 TZS/kg savings yields significant annual impact. Negotiate volume contracts with delivery schedules.
Est. impact: 30–50M TZS/year • Cost: Supplier negotiation only
4. Launch Ramadan/Low-Season Revenue Program High Impact — Before next Ramadan
February Ramadan caused a 12% network revenue drop (59M TZS lost in one month). GSM Plaza bucked this with +59% growth, suggesting location and menu matter. For next Ramadan: introduce iftar combo meals (family packs for evening breaking-fast), suhoor items (pre-dawn meal options), and delivery partnerships. Study what GSM Plaza did differently. Apply the same thinking to the general Feb trough.
Est. impact: 30–50M TZS/year (recovering half the seasonal dip) • Cost: Menu development + marketing
5. Replicate Kigamboni Model Across Network Strategic — Weeks 3–6
Kigamboni generates 3x the revenue of other branches (164M vs. 46–57M/month). Investigate what drives this: larger format? better location? extended hours? Identify the 2–3 replicable factors (likely menu breadth and seating capacity) and pilot them at underperforming branches. Even a 15% uplift to Masana and Sinza would yield meaningful gains.
Est. impact: 40–80M TZS/year if 2 branches improve 15% • Cost: Branch-specific investment
6. Cafe/Bakery Upsell Optimization Medium Impact — Weeks 2–4
K Cafe (bakery/coffee) contributes roughly 15–19% of branch revenue (visible in Masana data: ~6.5M of 42.4M in Jan 2025). Cafe items carry higher margins than chicken. Train counter staff on suggestive selling ("Would you like a coffee or pastry with that?"), introduce combo pricing, and improve cafe product visibility at point of sale. The cafe product line (coffee, tea, pastries) has near-zero waste in the data.
Est. impact: 20–40M TZS/year (15% uplift on cafe revenue across network) • Cost: Training + menu boards

Accounting Controls to Address

The cash settlement sheets show operating expenses (gas deliveries, salaries, potato purchases, maintenance) mixed in with customer payment records. This makes it difficult to distinguish true customer revenue from internal transfers. Separating operating disbursements from revenue collection into distinct tracking systems would improve financial visibility and audit readiness.
